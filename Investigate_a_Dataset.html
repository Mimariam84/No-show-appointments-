Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

PatientId Unique Number for the Patient AppointmentID Unique Number for the Appointment Gender indicates the patient gender ScheduledDay tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment. AppointmentDay tells us what is appointment date Age indicates the patient age Neighbourhood indicates the location of the hospital. Scholarship indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia. Hipertension indicates whether or not the patient is Hipertension Diabetes indicates whether or not the patient is Diabetes Alcoholism indicates whether or not the patient is Alcoholism Handcap indicates whether or not the patient is Handcap
SMS_received indicates whether or not the patient sent SMS No-show indicates whether or not the patientit showed up, it says No if the patient showed up to their appointment, and Yes if they did not show up.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

What is the % of patients who showed up vs the none-showed up ones? Is there the patient age affects the show up rate? Is there a correlation between the patient gender and the show up rate? Is the scholarship enrolment affect his presence in the scheduled appointments?

Conclusions¶

After Loading the data and explore it i have done some cleaning procedures which are:

  • checking for null values - Result : none
  • checking for duplicates - Result: none
  • Age column had and invalid values so i had to drop it.
  • Columns headers were in capital and lower case letters, i made them all lowe case.
  • no- show column header had a dash instead of underscore, and it is a highly used one so i replaced the dash with undescore;
  • There were some other cloumns that won't be used, so i dropped them all.

I started exploring data stage with ploting (created histograms) to all the columns to have an overall idea about the data then I started to work on each of my analysis questions which were:

1- What is the % of patients who show up vs the none show up ones? 2- Is the patient age affects show up rate? 3- What is the correlation between the patient gender and the show up rate? 4- Does the patientenrolment in scholarship affect his presence in the scheduled appointments?

Below were all the results concluded from analyzing the data:

Most of patients are not alcoholic, hand capped, diabetes, hypertension, not enrolled in scholarship and didn't send sms.

percentage of patients who show up 80.0 % percentage of patients who do not show p 20.0 %

It is more common that younger age patients don't show up in appointments, Highest No show up rate are patient between 20 and 60 years old.

No Correlation between Gender and Show up Rate

Since both scholarship enrolled patients and None scholarship enrolled patient are having high show up rate, then the scholarship is not an effective element in the patient appointment presence.

Limitations¶

I assumed that the value 0 for the scholarship column means that it is a yes and the 1 is a no.
